# Contributing to the Mycelium Plastic Degradation Protocol

## What this project is

An open-source experimental protocol for accelerating fungal biodegradation of synthetic polymers using:

- Monomer/oligomer pre-exposure (metabolic priming)
- Pulsed electromagnetic field stimulation
- Microbial consortia deployment

Published as Prior Art. No patents. Free for all humanity to use, test, and improve.

**Author:** Vitali Colesnicenco | fungal-priming-protocol
**Preprint:** [fungal-priming-protocol.md](./docs/fungal-priming-protocol.md)

## How you can help

### Priority 1 — Verify Phase 2 (EMF priming)

We are looking for laboratories to measure laccase expression levels in *Trametes versicolor* after:

- 10-minute pulsed EMF exposure at 900 MHz and 2.4 GHz
- In presence of 0.5% styrene monomer as primer
- Compared to control (no EMF, no primer)

**What to measure:** laccase activity (ABTS assay), total extracellular protein, mRNA expression levels at 16h and 24h post-exposure.

### Priority 2 — Monomer priming verification

Test whether pre-exposure to PE/PS/PET monomers or oligomers accelerates enzyme induction compared to direct polymer exposure. Any fungal species from the reference table in the preprint is valid.

### Priority 3 — Frequency mapping

Help build a systematic map of optimal EMF frequencies per fungal species. The preprint marks these as "TBD" — your data fills that gap.

### Priority 4 — Field deployment trials

If you have access to contaminated soil or landfill sites: test autonomous degradation activity of pre-primed mycelium vs. unprimed control after transfer to open environment.

## How to contribute results

1. Open an **Issue** in this repository with your experimental setup and results
2. Or submit a **Pull Request** adding your data to `/data/` folder
3. All contributors will be acknowledged in future versions of the preprint
